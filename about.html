Hello, I'm Madina

In 2019, I moved to Germany with my family. It was not an easy path — and still today, the road is full of challenges. Integrating into a new culture, learning a new language, understanding the social structure, and raising children has not been easy.

I originally come from Uzbekistan where I worked as a journalist. Later, I immigrated to Turkey and worked as a translator in a clinic. In Germany, I had to explore a completely new profession — and I chose programming.

Over a year ago, I began teaching myself programming, starting with Java. I wrestled with Spring, fell in love with databases — but I overlooked something: without practice and real work, all that hard-earned knowledge quickly fades. Still, I gained valuable experience — programming no longer feels scary. It's logical, explainable, and very interesting.

I couldn't break into a programming job. No experience — no job. No job — no experience. I was even offered to "fake experience" — but I couldn’t allow myself to do that.

Recently I started studying IAM and got very interested in AWS. It felt like a more realistic and approachable path than wrestling with code and algorithms. With AWS, many tools are already available — and I remembered that I used to manage forums and websites back in 2008–2011.

Now I’m building personal projects to show that I understand programming and that I’m capable of more — and this website is part of that.

I truly believe programming is about practice — the more you build, the more you know and the more you can.

Welcome to my story.
